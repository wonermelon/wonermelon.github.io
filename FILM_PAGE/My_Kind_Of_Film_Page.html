My Kind Of Film

The movies and TV shows listed here are my ultimate go-to choices for endless rewatches. They have a special place in my heart and never fail to uplift my spirits, especially during times when I feel low and uninspired. I always turn to these films for comfort and they work wonders in lifting my mood.

MOVIES

About Time (2013)

Synopsis:

          "About Time" is a romantic comedy-drama film released in 2013 and directed by Richard Curtis. The movie follows the story of Tim (Domhnall Gleeson), a young man who discovers that he has the ability to time travel. With this newfound power, he sets out to improve his love life by revisiting past moments and correcting mistakes. Along the way, he meets Mary (Rachel McAdams) and falls in love with her. As their relationship blossoms, Tim realizes that even with his ability to manipulate time, he cannot control everything. The film is a heartwarming tale of love, family, and the importance of cherishing every moment.

What I love about it:

          The film's unique concept of time travel used as a backdrop for a romantic story was both refreshing and engaging. The chemistry between the two lead actors, Domhnall Gleeson and Rachel McAdams, was palpable and their love story felt genuine and sincere. The movie had a perfect balance of humor, drama, and romance, and managed to deliver some profound insights about the importance of living in the present moment and cherishing the people we love. The supporting cast, particularly Bill Nighy as Tim's father, added to the emotional depth of the movie. Overall, About Time is a beautifully crafted film that will leave you feeling warm and inspired.

Before Sunset (2004)

Synopsis:

          "Before Sunset" is a romantic drama film released in 2004 and directed by Richard Linklater. It is the sequel to the 1995 film "Before Sunrise" and follows the story of Jesse (Ethan Hawke) and Celine (Julie Delpy), who reunite nine years after their initial encounter on a train in Europe. The film takes place in real-time as they spend an afternoon in Paris, walking and talking about their lives, relationships, and the choices they have made. As they revisit the past and explore their current feelings, Jesse and Celine grapple with the possibility of a second chance at love. The film is praised for its naturalistic dialogue, nuanced performances, and poignant exploration of love, regret, and the passage of time.

What I love about it:

          I absolutely loved the movie Before Sunset. The naturalistic dialogue and chemistry between the two main characters, Jesse and Celine, were the standout aspects for me. Their conversations felt authentic and spontaneous, as if I was eavesdropping on a real-life encounter. The film also tackled mature themes such as regret, missed opportunities, and the passage of time, which resonated with me deeply. The setting of Paris added to the romantic and nostalgic atmosphere of the film, making it a must-see for anyone who appreciates heartfelt and intelligent storytelling. Overall, Before Sunset is a beautiful and thought-provoking movie that I highly recommend.

The Longest Ride (2004)

Synopsis:

          "The Longest Ride" is based on the bestselling novel by master storyteller Nicholas Sparks and follows the star-crossed love affair of Luke, a former champion bull rider looking to make a comeback, and Sophia, a college student about to embark on her dream job in New York City's art world. As their relationship is tested by opposing paths and ideals, Sophia and Luke make an unexpected and fateful connection with Ira, whose memories of his own decades-long romance with his beloved wife deeply inspire the young couple. This movie spans generations and two intertwining love stories to explore the challenges and infinite rewards of enduring love.

What I love about it:

          What I loved about this movie is how it skillfully interweaves two love stories from different eras to create a captivating and heartwarming narrative. The film follows the love story of Luke, a bull rider, and Sophia, a college student who is interning in New York City. Alongside their modern-day romance, the film tells the tale of Ira and Ruth, an elderly couple who fell in love during the 1940s. The way these two stories intersect is both clever and moving, and the film does an excellent job of exploring the universal themes of love, sacrifice, and second chances. The acting is top-notch, with standout performances from the entire cast, and the breathtaking cinematography captures the beauty of the North Carolina mountains where the story takes place.

TV SHOWS

Hunter X Hunter (2004)

Synopsis:

          Hunter x Hunter is a popular Japanese anime series that follows the adventures of a young boy named Gon Freecss, who discovers that his father is a legendary Hunter, a member of a rare breed of skilled individuals who specialize in a wide range of dangerous activities. Determined to follow in his father's footsteps, Gon sets out on a journey to become a Hunter himself, meeting new friends and facing formidable foes along the way. The series features a unique power system called Nen, which allows users to manipulate their life energy to perform extraordinary feats, as well as complex and well-developed characters with compelling backstories. With its exciting action scenes, intricate plotlines, and emotional depth, Hunter x Hunter has become a beloved anime series among fans of the genre.

What I love about it:

          What I loved about Hunter x Hunter is how unique and captivating it is. One of the things that make the series stand out is its intricate power system called Nen, which allows characters to manipulate their life energy in creative and unexpected ways. The series also has a rich cast of characters, each with their own motivations and goals, and a compelling storyline that spans multiple story arcs. Whenever I want to distract myself from reality and feel like a kid again, I often rewatch Hunter x Hunter. Its imaginative world full of adventure and excitement never fails to transport me to another world, and I can't help but get lost in the show's captivating storytelling.

Mindhunter (2004)

Synopsis:

          "Mindhunter" is a Netflix original series that follows two FBI agents, Holden Ford and Bill Tench, as they interview imprisoned serial killers in the late 1970s and early 1980s. The goal of their interviews is to better understand the criminal mind and to develop techniques to help solve ongoing cases. As the series progresses, Holden and Bill's work becomes increasingly complex and challenging, as they begin to confront the darker aspects of their own psyches while pursuing some of the most notorious killers of their time. The series is known for its intense and nuanced exploration of the psychology of violent crime, as well as its portrayal of the toll that such work can take on the investigators themselves.

What I love about it:

          What I loved about the Netflix series "Mindhunter" is how it delves deeply into the psychology of violent crime and the minds of serial killers. The series is based on real-life events and characters, which adds to the authenticity of the show's portrayal of the FBI's early attempts to understand and catch serial killers. The performances by the lead actors, Jonathan Groff and Holt McCallany, are outstanding and add depth to their characters, who are both complex and flawed. The series is also notable for its atmospheric and moody cinematography, which creates a sense of tension and unease that keeps viewers on the edge of their seats. Overall, "Mindhunter" is a gripping and thought-provoking series that offers a unique insight into the criminal mind and the people who work to stop it.

Rick and Morty (2004)

Synopsis:

          Rick and Morty is a popular animated television series that follows the adventures of a mad scientist named Rick Sanchez and his good-hearted but often naive grandson Morty Smith. The show is known for its dark humor, satire, and sci-fi elements, as Rick and Morty travel through different dimensions and alternate universes, encountering bizarre creatures and facing dangerous situations along the way. The series explores themes such as family, love, and existentialism, often presenting complex and philosophical concepts in a comedic and approachable way. Despite its animated format, Rick and Morty has gained a loyal fanbase and critical acclaim for its intelligent writing, creative storytelling, and unique style.

What I love about it:

          One of the aspects that I loved and what make Rick and Morty unique is its ability to combine dark humor with complex and thought-provoking storytelling. The show is not afraid to tackle existentialist themes, such as the meaning of life, free will, and the nature of reality, while still maintaining a sense of comedic irreverence. Additionally, the series explores different dimensions and alternate universes, presenting a rich and imaginative sci-fi world that is both fascinating and absurd. The show also features flawed and complex characters, particularly Rick, who serves as both an anti-hero and a tragic figure. Overall, Rick and Morty's blend of humor, sci-fi, and philosophical depth makes it a one-of-a-kind viewing experience that has captured the hearts of people.

She Was Pretty (2004)

Synopsis:

          "She Was Pretty" is a Korean drama series that follows the story of Kim Hye-jin, a formerly beautiful and popular girl who has fallen on hard times. After losing her job, Hye-jin reconnects with her childhood friend, Ji Sung-joon, who has become a successful and handsome magazine editor. However, Hye-jin is now unrecognizable from her former self, and in order to maintain their friendship, she pretends to be her attractive best friend, Ha Ri. As the story unfolds, Hye-jin and Sung-joon become close again, and Hye-jin begins to develop feelings for him, despite the fact that he is in love with Ha Ri. The series explores themes of friendship, love, and self-discovery, and features a talented cast of actors who bring the characters to life with humor, heart, and plenty of drama.

What I love about it:

          What I loved about this series is how it explores the themes of self-discovery, self-acceptance, and the true meaning of beauty. It tackles these themes with humor, heart, and plenty of drama, and the talented cast brings the characters to life in a way that is both relatable and entertaining. The chemistry between the two leads, Hwang Jung-eum and Park Seo-joon, is palpable, and their on-screen romance is both sweet and heart-wrenching. The series also features a strong supporting cast, including Go Joon-hee and Choi Si-won, who provide plenty of comedic relief and emotional depth.
